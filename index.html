About Me

Hello! I am a dedicated PhD student in Biostatistics with a passion for improving health research through data analysis. My work focuses on using data from wearable devices to study important health issues. By examining real-world data, I aim to find patterns and insights that can help create better and more personalized health solutions. Since wearable device data can sometimes contain errors, I am expanding my research to address these challenges. I am developing reliable methods to identify and correct these errors, ensuring that the results are as accurate as possible. This focus on both practical health applications and strong research methods drives my commitment to making a meaningful impact in public health and biostatistics.
